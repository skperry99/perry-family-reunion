In Loving Memory

As we gather for the first Perry Family Reunion, we also hold close the family members who are no longer with us. Their stories, humor, hard work, and love helped shape who we are today.

This page is a small way to honor them. During the reunion, we’ll have additional space to share memories, photos, and stories together.

Irja “Irene” M. (Hakala) Perry

Irja “Irene” Marie Hakala Perry was born on February 4, 1911 in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula, the daughter of Finnish immigrants Jacob and Maria (Oja) Hakala. She married William Henry Perry in October 1939, and together they raised nine children and built a life rooted in faith, family, and hard work in the U.P.

Irene lived a long life, passing away at the age of 95 on March 30, 2006, surrounded by family. The values she carried from her Finnish upbringing—perseverance, hospitality, and quiet strength—still ripple through her children, grandchildren, and great-grandchildren.

At the reunion, we remember her as “Mom,” “Grandma,” and “Great-Grandma Irene,” whose kitchen, stories, and hugs were at the center of so many early Perry memories.

John Alan Perry

John Alan Perry, one of William and Irene’s sons, was born on November 28, 1942, in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula. He passed away on September 20, 2025, at the age of 82, in Maryland Heights, Missouri.

John was a son, brother, dad, grandpa, and friend. For many of us, he is remembered for his wit, his stories, and the way he could fill a room with conversation. His life connected the U.P. roots of the Perry family to later chapters in Illinois and Missouri, and to children and grandchildren who carry his memory forward.

During the reunion weekend, we will set aside time for a Celebration of Life in John’s honor, along with time to remember other loved ones who have passed. This will be a space for stories, photos, and quiet moments of gratitude.

Remembering Others

In addition to John and Irene, four of the nine Perry children and many other relatives have passed away over the years. Each of them—parents, siblings, cousins, spouses, and friends—has left an imprint on our family.

If you would like someone mentioned by name, or if you have a favorite photo you’d like included in a slideshow or memory table, please reach out to the reunion organizer before the weekend.
