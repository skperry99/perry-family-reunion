In Loving Memory

We lovingly remember William Henry Perry Sr. and Irja “Irene” (Hakala) Perry, along with their children and other family members who are no longer with us. Their stories, humor, and kindness live on in all of us.

William & Irene Perry

William Henry Perry Sr. (1910–1970) was born in Crystal Falls, Michigan, the son of Cornish immigrants Andrew and Anna Perry. He worked in the Upper Peninsula mining industry and made his home in Ishpeming.

Irja “Irene” M. (Hakala) Perry (1911–2006) was born in Princeton Location, Michigan, to Finnish immigrants Jacob and Maria (Oja) Hakala. William and Irene married in October 1939 in Ishpeming and went on to raise nine children between 1939 and 1952. Their family later spread from Michigan to Missouri, Florida, and beyond, but the roots trace back to their home in the U.P.

Remembering Their Children

William H. “Bill” Perry Jr. (1939–2015)

Born in Ishpeming and raised in the U.P., Bill later made his home in Midland, Michigan. He worked for Dow Chemical as a pipefitter/multi-craftsman for 30 years, where coworkers knew him as “the Admiral.” After retiring, he continued to work part-time and remained active at Trinity Lutheran Church. He was known for his quiet humor, steady presence, and deep devotion to his wife, children, grandchildren, nieces, and nephews.

Garry B. Perry (1944–2007)

Garry built a career as a certified public accountant in Florida, spending more than two decades with a Lakeland CPA firm before moving to Illinois to serve as chief financial officer for his brother’s company. He made his home in Tampa with his wife, Kathy, and their children, Kirstan and Schaler. Garry is remembered for his work ethic, dry wit, and love for his family.

John Alan Perry (1942–2025)

John was born in Negaunee, Michigan, and carried his U.P. roots with him through a life that eventually led to Maryland Heights, Missouri. He was a son, brother, dad, grandpa, and uncle, and his life bridged Michigan, Illinois, and Missouri. At our 2026 reunion we’ll gather for a Celebration of Life to share stories, photos, and memories of John and the many ways he made us laugh and feel at home.

Elizabeth “Liz” Ann Perry (1950–2018)

Liz was born in 1950 and spent much of her life in Michigan, later living in Lansing. She is remembered as a loving daughter, sister, aunt, and friend. Though some of us knew her well and others only through stories, her place in the Perry family tree is cherished, and we honor her memory alongside her parents and brothers.
